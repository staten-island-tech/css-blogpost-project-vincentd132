Seals

Seals are intriguing marine mammals known for their playful nature and streamlined bodies, which make them agile swimmers. They come in various species, such as the harbor seal and elephant seal, each adapted to different environments. Social animals, they often live in large colonies and communicate with vocalizations and body language. While their diet includes fish and squid, many seal species face threats from climate change and human activities, highlighting the need for conservation. More Info

Orcas

Orcas, or killer whales, are the largest dolphins, recognized by their black-and-white coloring and intelligence. Found in oceans globally, they live in social pods and use complex vocalizations for communication and hunting. Their diet varies widely, and they play a crucial role in marine ecosystems. More Info

Great White Shark

The great white shark is a formidable predator known for its size, speed, and powerful jaws. Found in coastal waters worldwide, this apex predator can grow up to 20 feet long and weigh over 5,000 pounds. Great whites are distinguished by their robust, torpedo-shaped bodies and sharp, serrated teeth, which they use to hunt a range of prey, from seals to large fish. Despite their fearsome reputation, great white sharks are vital to marine ecosystems, helping to regulate the populations of other species and maintain balance in their environments. More Info
